1. What Data Do Social Networks Collect?

Platforms such as Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, WhatsApp, and X collect far more than just your posts.

  • Location data (GPS, IP address)
  • Browsing history and interactions
  • Contacts and metadata
  • Behavioral preferences
  • Biometric data (depending on the platform)

This data is used for targeted advertising, algorithm optimization, and in some cases advanced behavioral profiling.

2. What Is a Digital Footprint?

A digital footprint is the collection of traces you leave online: posts, comments, tags, and passive data gathered through trackers.

It can influence:

  • Professional recruitment opportunities
  • Social reputation
  • Personal security
  • Commercial profiling

3. App Permissions: What Are You Really Granting?

Many applications request unnecessary access to your microphone, contacts, storage, or camera.

For example, a photo editing app typically has no legitimate reason to access your contact list.

Regularly review:

  • Settings > Privacy > App Permissions
  • Revoke unnecessary access
  • Remove suspicious applications

4. Cookies and Advertising Tracking

Cookies are small files stored in your browser that allow websites to track your activity.

They explain why advertisements appear shortly after you search for something online.

Methods of tracking:

  • Third-party cookies
  • Tracking pixels
  • Digital fingerprinting

Use tracker blockers and limit third-party cookies in your browser settings.

5. Privacy Settings by Social Network

Essential settings to activate:

  • Private account mode
  • Two-factor authentication (2FA)
  • Comment restrictions
  • Tag review and control
  • Connected app management

6. Public vs. Private Accounts

Public account: maximum visibility, increased exposure.

Private account: greater control, reduced reach.

The decision depends on your goals: professional visibility or personal protection.

7. Strong Passwords and Two-Factor Authentication

  • Use long, unique passwords
  • Consider a password manager
  • Enable two-step verification (2FA)
  • Avoid reusing credentials

8. What Happens to Your Data When You Delete an Account?

Deleting an app does not necessarily mean your data is deleted.

Some platforms retain archived data for months.

In many cases, you must submit a formal request for permanent data deletion.

9. Practical Tips for Responsible Social Media Use

  • Think carefully before posting
  • Limit publicly visible personal information
  • Review privacy settings every 3–6 months
  • Stay informed about digital security risks

10. Useful Websites for Social Media & Cybersecurity

  • Canadian Centre for Cyber Security – Official Canadian government cybersecurity guidance and alerts.
  • Have I Been Pwned – Check if your email or password has been compromised in a data breach.
  • StaySafeOnline – Practical advice for users and businesses on cybersecurity.
  • Common Sense Education – Guides for responsible social media use and digital citizenship.
  • Electronic Frontier Foundation (EFF) – Privacy tools, tips, and online safety resources.
